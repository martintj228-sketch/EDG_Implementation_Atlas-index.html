Implementation narrative

The EDG model is not over-engineered. It emphasizes controlled go-live behavior with room to relax controls later.

  • Centralized accounting remains the anchor operating assumption.
  • Project accounting is the bridge between operations, billing, payroll/job cost, and reporting.
  • Controls such as hold-on-entry are intentionally conservative during rollout, not necessarily permanent.
  • Migration philosophy favors clean cutover control over loading unnecessary historical detail into day-one operations.

Module decision cards

This section uses a decision-card presentation similar to the reference structure, but mapped to EDG’s actual implementation choices.

GL

Financial backbone is established

Entity structure, branch design, and the future-state chart of accounts are all sufficiently defined to support downstream configuration.

Projects

Project accounting is the main design spine

Project-to-Cost-Code structure and Account Groups are the primary basis for cost collection, billing logic, and reporting.

AP

AP starts in controlled mode

Vendor credits age in AP, retainage is in scope, and entry controls stay on initially while approval routing still needs to be finalized.

AR

AR credits remain manual and visible

Customer credits stay open until selected, but AR credits are not aged; hold-on-entry also stays on for early rollout.

Payroll / Miter

Miter owns payroll-source behavior

Miter remains the employee, time, payroll, scheduling, and employee-expense source system and will push summary payroll/job cost into Acumatica.

Tax / policy

Tax and control refinements remain open

Sales tax is manual at go-live, but use-tax design, long-term control policy, and some approval logic are explicitly deferred.
